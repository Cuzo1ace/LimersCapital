## Delivery notes (not part of the email body)

### Word count
~340 words. Under the 350 target.

### Channel recommendation
**Primary: Twitter DM to `@shrikant_db`**. Shri is the only team member with a public Twitter handle. DMs are lower-friction than cold email and the tone above reads well in DM format. Paste the email body directly; the subject line is only needed if you fall back to email.

**Not recommended**: GitHub issue, LinkedIn, or any channel that makes the outreach look like spam or a sales pitch. The framing "founder-to-founder learning" only works in founder-native channels.

### Tone calibration
- **Warm, not formal.** No "Dear Mr. Shrikant." Founder-to-founder peer tone.
- **Show the homework, don't brag about it.** "I saw Credible Finance v1 at Renaissance and then the v2 pivot at Cypherpunk" is enough to prove you did the research. Do not list all the data points from the Copilot search.
- **Lead with the structural analog.** The opening "closest structural analog" line is the whole email's hook.
- **Honest ask.** "30 minutes of your time" is specific and small. Don't dress it up as anything bigger.
- **Reciprocity.** The "happy to trade" paragraph is critical — it prevents the email from reading as a one-way extraction.
- **Don't mention Colosseum Copilot** — it's your private research tool, and Shri doesn't need to know how you found them. Keep it as "reading the Colosseum corpus."

### Timing
- **Send on a Tuesday or Wednesday morning Caribbean time** (Tue/Wed 08:00–10:00 AST = Tue/Wed 17:30–19:30 IST).
- **Avoid Monday mornings** (founders clearing inbox, low attention) and Fridays (low response rate).

### Attachments
Do NOT attach files to the initial DM/email — they trigger spam heuristics on cold outreach.

If Shri replies positively and asks for more context, send in the follow-up:
1. `docs/Limers-Capital-TTSE-Tokenization-One-Pager-Branded.pdf` (the one-pager you had me build earlier)

### After sending — what to track
- **Did he open it?** (Twitter shows "seen" indicator for DMs)
- **Did he reply within 72 hours?** If yes → schedule immediately.
- **Did he reply within 5 days?** If yes but no call scheduled → follow up with the template below.
- **No reply by day 5?** Follow up once, then leave it. Don't triple-message.
