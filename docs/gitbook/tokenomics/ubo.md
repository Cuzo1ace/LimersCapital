# Universal Basic Ownership

Universal Basic Ownership (UBO) is Limer's Capital founding economic model -- a commitment that the platform's value accrues to its community, not to extractive intermediaries.

## Core Principles

1. **50% of tokens to the community** -- airdrops, LP mining, referrals, and education rewards ensure the majority of $LIMER ends up in users' hands
2. **50% of revenue to stakers** -- protocol revenue is distributed in USDC and SOL, never in $LIMER, to avoid circular dilution
3. **Education is always free** -- enshrined in the irrevocable Protocol Charter
4. **Max 25% voting power per entity** -- no single wallet, fund, or insider can capture governance

## The Protocol Charter

The Protocol Charter is an on-chain, irrevocable governance document that locks these commitments permanently. Key provisions:

- Free education cannot be removed by any governance vote
- Revenue share ratio (50%) cannot be reduced below 40%
- Voting power cap (25%) cannot be increased
- Charter amendments require 67% supermajority with a 30-day timelock

## Revenue Distribution

Stakers receive their share of protocol revenue in stablecoins and SOL:

| Revenue Source | Staker Share |
|---------------|-------------|
| Trading fees | 50% in USDC |
| Subscription revenue | 50% in USDC |
| API fees | 50% in SOL |

## The Diaspora as Shareholders

6 million Caribbean-born people live abroad, sending $20B home annually at a 7% average fee. UBO transforms diaspora members from remittance senders into equity holders -- owning a stake in the Caribbean's financial infrastructure rather than paying fees to intermediaries.
