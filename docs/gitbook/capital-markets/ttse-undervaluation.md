# TTSE Undervaluation Analysis

The Trinidad & Tobago Stock Exchange trades at a structural discount to every comparable market. This page quantifies the gap and maps how tokenization closes it.

## PE Ratio Comparison

| Market | PE Ratio | Discount vs TTSE |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| **TTSE (Trinidad)** | **9.9x** | -- |
| JSE (Jamaica) | 13.4x | TTSE is 26% cheaper |
| Emerging Markets avg | 17.0x | TTSE is 42% cheaper |
| S&P 500 (US) | 21.0x | TTSE is 53% cheaper |

## Five Structural Causes

| Cause | Impact | Description |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| Illiquidity | 30% | Average daily volume under $500K; wide bid-ask spreads |
| Market fragmentation | 25% | 7 Caribbean exchanges with no interoperability |
| No institutional access | 20% | Foreign investors cannot easily participate |
| Information asymmetry | 15% | Limited analyst coverage, no real-time data feeds |
| Settlement friction | 10% | T+3 settlement, manual processes, paper certificates |

## Company-Level Evidence

ANSA McAL -- the largest conglomerate in the Caribbean -- trades at approximately 72 cents on the dollar relative to its book value. Republic Bank, the region's leading financial institution, trades at 8.5x PE versus JPMorgan at 12x for comparable metrics.

## How Tokenization Solves Each Cause

Tokenized securities on Solana provide 24/7 liquidity, cross-border access, instant settlement (400ms), transparent on-chain order books, and fractional ownership -- directly addressing all five structural causes.

A PE re-rating from 9.9x to the EM average of 17x implies approximately $8.9B in unlocked market capitalization across the TTSE. Even a partial re-rating to 13x (Jamaica-level) unlocks $4.2B.
