# Global vs Local Comparisons

Caribbean companies operate at world-class scale but trade at steep discounts to global peers. These comparisons illustrate the structural undervaluation across sectors.

## Head-to-Head Comparisons

### Technology & Conglomerates

| Metric | Apple (AAPL) | ANSA McAL (AMCL) |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| PE Ratio | 28x | 10x |
| Dividend Yield | 0.5% | 3.2% |
| Insight | ANSA operates across 6 Caribbean nations in manufacturing, financial services, and retail -- yet trades at a 64% PE discount to Apple |

### Banking & Financial Services

| Metric | JPMorgan (JPM) | Republic Bank (RFHL) |
|--------|----------------|---------------------|
| PE Ratio | 12x | 8.5x |
| Dividend Yield | 2.4% | 4.1% |
| Insight | Republic Bank serves 6 Caribbean countries with strong ROE and conservative lending -- yet trades at a 29% PE discount with nearly double the yield |

### Market Index Comparison

| Metric | S&P 500 | TTSE Composite |
|--------|---------|----------------|
| PE Ratio | 21x | 9.9x |
| Dividend Yield | 1.3% | 3.8% |
| Insight | The TTSE composite trades at less than half the S&P 500 PE while delivering 3x the dividend yield -- a classic value opportunity hidden by illiquidity |

## The Pattern

Across every sector, Caribbean companies deliver higher yields at lower valuations. The discount is not driven by fundamentals but by structural market limitations that tokenization can resolve.
