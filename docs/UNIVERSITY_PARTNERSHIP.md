# University Partnership Proposal — Financial Literacy Curriculum

**Last updated:** April 7, 2026

---

## 1. Value Proposition

Limer's Capital offers Caribbean universities a **free, interactive financial literacy curriculum** covering blockchain, Caribbean markets, DeFi strategies, and portfolio management.

### What We Provide

- **8 modules** spanning beginner to advanced topics
- **37 lessons** with interactive content and real market data
- **8 quizzes** with server-validated assessment
- **Gamified progression** — XP, badges, and skill tracking keep students engaged
- **Curriculum Mode** — a stripped-down interface designed for classroom use (no trading, no wallet requirement)
- **Caribbean-specific content** — TTSE integration, regional regulation mapping, CBDC education

### Cost to University

**Free.** No licensing fees, no per-student costs, no contracts. The platform is free for educational use as guaranteed by the [Protocol Charter](PROTOCOL_CHARTER.md), Article II.

## 5. Student Onboarding

### Cohort Setup

1. University contact receives a **cohort referral code** (e.g., `UWI-MONA-2026`)
2. Students enter the code when they first access the platform
3. Each student earns 200 LP (referral bonus) + the university cohort gets tracked as a group

### Learning Path

```
Curriculum Mode (Weeks 1-12)
  → Complete all 8 modules + quizzes
  → Earn XP + badges + LP throughout
  → No trading UI, no wallet required

Full Platform Access (Post-Course)
  → Students toggle to full mode
  → Connect wallet (optional)
  → Paper trading with skills learned
  → LP accumulated during course converts to $LIMER at airdrop
```

### Assessment Rubrics

Each lesson includes `curriculum.learningObjectives` and `curriculum.assessmentRubric` metadata with three levels:
- **Excellent** — Demonstrates mastery with application to Caribbean context
- **Proficient** — Understands core concepts and can identify key examples
- **Developing** — Recognizes terminology but needs further practice

## 6. Implementation Guide for Professors

### Classroom Setup

1. Students navigate to `limerscapital.com`
2. Click "Curriculum Mode" toggle on the Learn page
3. No account creation needed — progress saves to browser
4. Professor can project the platform and walk through lessons together

### Assessment Options

- **Platform quizzes** — built-in, auto-graded, server-validated
- **Discussion questions** — each lesson includes Caribbean context prompts
- **Project work** — students can analyze TTSE stocks, compare Caribbean regulations, or design LP strategies
- **Peer teaching** — students who complete modules early can mentor classmates (builds community)
